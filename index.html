PROCESS

Discovery

I start by asking questions - a lot of them. Who is this for? What are the goals of this project? What is the scope? Are we building entirely from scratch or working with an existing brand? This helps establish a personality and the constraints for the project and will set the tone for the following steps.

Research

I take time to understand the industry and competition. Then, I find sources to get inspired from. I flood myself with a variety of inspiration, but take care to put that inspiration away when it comes time for execution. This is so I can come up with an informed yet tailored, unique solution without being too derivative.

Content

I learn the tone of the copy and prioritize information that is most important. Having a background in marketing copywriting in addition to design comes in handy for me here.

Sketching

All of previous steps are translated into iterations on paper while I find the best flow for the site. I will then translate the more successful of sketches into wireframes. Wireframes are great for user testing - and here we'll work out any kinks in the design.

Mockups

The wireframes are brought to life in mockups, which will be presented for feedback. Photoshop Etiquette is my bible. I strive to create easily navigable files with the development process in mind.

Code

When mockups are approved, I hammer out code that makes the design work or hand it off to a developer.

ABOUT

Hi there! I’m a multi disciplinary designer from the Orlando, FL area. Although in the past I’ve focused primarily in print, I’ve fallen in love with digital. My current areas of focus are identity design, web design, illustration and front end development.

When I’m not designing, I enjoy getting away from the computer and creating analog artwork by using pastels, gouache and other mixed media. I also recharge by biking, practicing yoga and playing with my two rescue pups.
